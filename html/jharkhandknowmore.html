Welcome to Jharkhand

Jharkhand is a treasure trove of natural beauty, lying mostly on the Chota Nagpur Plateau. Known for its mineral wealth and dense forests, its tourism revolves around breathtaking waterfalls, pristine wildlife sanctuaries, and some of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India. Get ready to explore the serene heart of Eastern India!

Top Tourist Destinations

Ranchi - The City of Waterfalls

The capital city is famous for its numerous cascades. Key attractions include Hundru Falls (dropping 320 ft), Dassam Falls, Jagannath Temple (a replica of the Puri temple), and Tagore Hill.

Deoghar - Spiritual Hub

A sacred destination for pilgrims, housing the Baba Baidyanath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India. Also visit Trikut Hill and Basukinath Temple.

Netarhat - Queen of Chotanagpur

A stunning hill station known for its magnificent sunrises and sunsets, especially from Magnolia Point. Nearby attractions include Lodh Falls (the highest in Jharkhand).

Jamshedpur - The Steel City

India's first planned industrial city, offering a mix of urban development and nature. Visit Jubilee Park, Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary, and the serene Dimna Lake.

Wildlife, History & Pilgrimage

Natural Wonders

  • Patratu Valley & Dam: Breathtaking winding roads, panoramic views, and the large artificial reservoir.
  • Jonha Falls: Known as Gautam Dhara, accessible by descending 500 steps.
  • Usri Falls (Giridih): Gushing cascade from the Parasnath Hills.
  • Maithon Lake (Dhanbad): A serene reservoir on the Barakar River, excellent for boating and picnics.
  • Dimna Lake (Jamshedpur): A scenic lake at the foothills of Dalma Hills, popular picnic spot.

Sacred Destinations

  • Parasnath Hill (Shikharji): The most important Jain pilgrimage site globally.
  • Rajrappa (Chhinnamasta Temple): A famous Shakti Peeth located at the confluence of the Damodar and Bhera rivers.
  • Maluti Village (Dumka): Known for its cluster of 72 ancient terracotta temples.
  • Dewri Temple (Ranchi): Known for its 16-armed idol of Goddess Durga.
  • Harihar Dham (Giridih): Famous for having one of the largest Shiva Lingams in the world.

Safaris & Heritage

  • Betla National Park: Famous for wildlife, including tigers, and ancient Palamau Forts (ruins of the Chero dynasty) located within its boundaries.
  • Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary: Famous for its population of wild elephants.
  • Tribal Research Institute (Ranchi): Museum offering a deep insight into the culture and history of Jharkhand's indigenous tribes.
  • Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary: A peaceful retreat for nature lovers and bird watchers.
  • Navratangarh (Gumla): Ruins of the former capital of the Nagvanshi dynasty.
