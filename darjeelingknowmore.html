Discover Darjeeling

The Queen of the Hills

A Glimpse into the Majestic Hill Station

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Darjeeling is a mesmerizing blend of breathtaking landscapes, lush tea plantations, and vibrant culture. From stunning sunrises to charming toy train rides, it's a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

Tiger Hill

Witness the magical sunrise over Kanchenjunga.

Tiger Hill is world-renowned for its panoramic views of the sunrise, painting the snow-capped peaks of Mount Kanchenjunga in a spectacular array of colors. On a clear day, you can even spot Mount Everest in the distance. A visit here is an early morning ritual for every tourist.

Darjeeling Toy Train

A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1999.

A nostalgic ride on this narrow-gauge railway is a must. The Toy Train chugs its way through misty valleys, lush tea gardens, and charming villages, offering an unforgettable journey through the heart of Darjeeling's stunning landscape.

Batasia Loop

A spiral railway marvel with a war memorial.

This is an engineering masterpiece where the Toy Train takes a 360-degree loop to navigate a steep descent. Located amidst a beautifully manicured garden with a war memorial, it offers a perfect vantage point for a stunning view of Darjeeling town and the surrounding mountains.

Peace Pagoda

A symbol of peace and harmony.

Built by a Japanese Buddhist order, this beautiful white stupa is a serene sanctuary dedicated to world peace. The site also provides a spectacular panoramic view of the town and the snow-capped Himalayan peaks, making it a place of tranquility and reflection.

Happy Valley Tea Estate

Explore one of the oldest tea estates.

A visit to Darjeeling is incomplete without experiencing its famous tea culture. Happy Valley Tea Estate, one of the oldest in the region, offers guided tours where you can learn about the tea-making process, from plucking to processing, and even enjoy a fresh cup of world-famous Darjeeling tea.

Zoological Park

Home to endangered Himalayan species.

This high-altitude zoo is a haven for wildlife lovers, home to rare and endangered species like the Red Panda, Snow Leopard, and Tibetan Wolf. It's a key center for conservation and breeding programs, offering an educational and memorable experience for visitors of all ages.

Travel Tips for Your Trip

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit is from April to June for pleasant weather, or from September to November for clear views of the mountains. Avoid the monsoon season (July-August) due to heavy rainfall.

What to Wear

Pack warm clothes, including jackets, sweaters, and a scarf, as the weather can be chilly, especially during early mornings and evenings. Comfortable walking shoes are a must for exploring the town.

Local Cuisine

Don't leave without trying the local delicacies! Momos (steamed dumplings), Thukpa (noodle soup), and the famous Darjeeling tea are a treat for your taste buds.
