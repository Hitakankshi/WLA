Top Tourist Destinations

UNESCO Heritage

Khajuraho Temples

World-renowned for its group of Hindu and Jain temples built by the Chandela dynasty. Famous for intricate and elaborate carvings, including the iconic erotic sculptures, showcasing medieval Indian artistry.

Wildlife Safari

Kanha National Park

One of the largest National Parks in Central India and a premier tiger reserve. It is known for its high population of Royal Bengal Tigers and the rare Hard-ground Barasingha.

Historical Landmark

Gwalior Fort

A massive hilltop fortress dubbed the 'Gibraltar of India.' It houses several temples, palaces, and water tanks, reflecting centuries of dynastic rule and architectural evolution.

Buddhist Monument

Sanchi Stupa

Commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd Century BCE, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is the oldest stone structure in India and a masterpiece of Buddhist art and architecture.

Hill Station

Pachmarhi

Known as the 'Queen of Satpura,' Pachmarhi is the only hill station in MP. It is famous for its serene waterfalls (like Bee Falls), caves, and lush green Satpura National Park.

Pilgrimage Site

Ujjain

One of the seven sacred Hindu cities, Ujjain is home to the famous Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, one of the 12 most revered shrines of Lord Shiva. It is also a site for the Kumbh Mela.

Natural Wonder

Bhedaghat & Marble Rocks

Located near Jabalpur, this spot is famous for the stunning 100-foot-tall marble rocks flanking the Narmada River. Also home to the majestic Dhuandhar Falls (Smoke Cascade).

Riverside Town

Orchha

A historical town on the banks of the Betwa River, famous for its magnificent Orchha Fort Complex, Jehangir Mahal, and the unique Ram Raja Temple where Lord Rama is worshipped as a King.
