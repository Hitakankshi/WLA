Welcome to Odisha, a state rich in ancient temples, vibrant culture, and pristine natural beauty. Explore its hidden gems and timeless traditions.

Must-Visit Places

Puri: The Abode of Lord Jagannath

A major pilgrimage site, Puri is home to the world-famous Jagannath Temple, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. The city is also known for its stunning golden beach and vibrant Rath Yatra festival.

Konark: The Sun Temple Marvel

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Konark Sun Temple is an architectural masterpiece designed as a giant chariot. Its intricate stone carvings and historical significance are a treat for history and art lovers.

Bhubaneswar: The Temple City

Known for its ancient temples, Bhubaneswar is a perfect blend of history and modernity. The Lingaraj Temple, Mukteswar Temple, and Rajarani Temple are architectural wonders, while the city's parks and museums offer a contemporary feel.

Chilika Lake: Asia's Largest Lagoon

A haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers, Chilika Lake is a vast brackish water lagoon. It's a paradise for migratory birds and home to the endangered Irrawaddy dolphins.

Daringbadi: The Kashmir of Odisha

Escape the heat at this charming hill station in the Kandhamal district. With its picturesque valleys, pine forests, and cool climate, it's a perfect retreat for those seeking tranquility.

Bhitarkanika: A Mangrove Paradise

As India's second-largest mangrove ecosystem, Bhitarkanika National Park is a biodiversity hotspot. It's a major nesting site for saltwater crocodiles and Olive Ridley sea turtles.

Culture & Festivals

Dance & Music

Odisha is the land of Odissi, one of the eight classical dance forms of India. The state also has a rich tradition of folk dances like Chhau and Gotipua. The annual Konark Dance Festival is a spectacular event showcasing India's classical dance heritage.

Festivals

The state comes alive with festivals. The most famous is the Rath Yatra in Puri, where the deities of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are carried in grand chariots. Other major festivals include Durga Puja, Konark Dance Festival, and Bali Jatra.

Your Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Odisha is during the winter months from October to March. The weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and exploring the temples and beaches. The monsoon season (July to September) makes the landscapes lush and green, while summers (April to June) can be very hot, though they are ideal for visiting hill stations like Daringbadi.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Biju Patnaik International Airport (BBI) in Bhubaneswar is well-connected to major cities.
  • By Rail: The state has a strong rail network. Bhubaneswar and Puri are major railway junctions.
  • By Road: Odisha is well-connected by a network of National and State Highways. Buses and taxis are readily available.

Local Cuisine

Don't leave without tasting the local flavors! Try Pakhala Bhata (fermented rice), Dalma (lentils with vegetables), and sweets like Chhena Poda (burnt cheese cake) and Rasagola. Fresh seafood is a must-try in coastal areas like Puri and Gopalpur.
